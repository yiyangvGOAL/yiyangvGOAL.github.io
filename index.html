Research

Agent programming languages · formal verification · safe autonomous decision-making.

I specialize in agent programming languages, formal verification, and safe autonomous decision-making — designing logic-based frameworks with formally defined syntax and semantics that let autonomous systems specify and enforce machine-readable safety constraints.

My path into formal methods began with interactive theorem proving. During my MSc at RWTH Aachen, I extended MontiBelle, an Isabelle/HOL & HOLCF framework for distributed cyber-physical systems, defining new continuous stream operators and proving around 80 lemmas to enable the verification of stateful automata.

During my PhD at KU Leuven, I developed vGOAL, an agent programming language and framework that integrates runtime safety enforcement with automated verification pipelines based on CTL and PCTL model checking. This work supports verifiably safe multi-agent systems and has been validated on real-world robotic platforms.

My current research extends these foundations toward trustworthy and responsible AI, including LLM-supported decision-making under normative constraints. I am particularly interested in bridging formal methods with practical AI systems to ensure safety, reliability, and human-centered behavior in autonomous and interactive environments.
